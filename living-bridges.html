A definition

A guide shows the way from outside. A Living Bridge is the way — someone who has crossed, who carries the memory of both shores in their body, and who offers their hand to those still standing at the water's edge.

"They speak your language, they know your fears, they carry the same wound. They are not here to teach you. They are the proof that the return is possible."

A Living Bridge is not a Babalawo, not a therapist, not a salesperson. They have received initiation — Fâ, Vodoun — and they have undergone the return path as described in the Ayo Traditions doctrine. They are, in the precise words of that doctrine, someone who has begun their own return and now holds a lantern for others. No more than that. No less.

Not this

A guru or spiritual authority telling you what to believe or do.

This

An elder sibling — further along on this specific path, not above you on any other.

Not this

A therapist or life coach offering diagnosis, advice, or solutions.

This

A witness — someone who sees you without judging, and reflects you back to yourself.

Not this

A representative of Ayo Traditions selling programmes or pushing an agenda.

This

A threshold — the living in-between, where one world can meet another, safely.

Ifashola Ayuso

From Belize · Now in Porto-Novo

English

For fifteen years, Ifashola traveled between the United States and the Gulf of Guinea, searching for a truth that neither world could fully give her. She knew the ache of the diaspora — the feeling of being at home nowhere and everywhere, of carrying a memory she could not name.

In Porto-Novo, she found what she was looking for. She received her Fâ, was initiated into Vodoun, and finally recognized the face of her Orí. Today, she no longer chases miracles. She walks the path of remembrance — and accompanies those who seek not quick answers, but a true direction for their lives.

If you carry the same questions she once carried, Ifashola offers a free 30-minute conversation. No pressure, no sales — just a human exchange, a mirror, a door.

Amara Carvalho

De Salvador, Bahia · Agora em Porto-Novo

Português English

Amara nasceu dentro de um terreiro em Salvador — o candomblé corria pela sua infância como uma segunda língua. Mas quando se mudou para São Paulo, depois para a Europa, o fio foi se afunilando. Durante anos, ela viveu entre mundos, carregando uma tradição que sentia não ter mais o direito de habitar plenamente.

A pergunta que a trouxe a Porto-Novo era simples: de onde vêm os orixás? Ela chegou por um mês. Ficou. Recebeu seu Fâ. Foi iniciada no Vodoun. E compreendeu, enfim, que o que ela havia carregado em Salvador nunca foi uma cópia diminuída de algo — era a mesma raiz, em solo diferente.

Amara fala para brasileiros e afrodescendentes lusófonos que sentem o chamado da tradição mas perderam o fio — ou que nunca souberam que havia um fio para encontrar.

Mariétou Diouf

De Paris · Maintenant à Porto-Novo

Français English Wolof

Mariétou grandit entre Dakar et Paris — fille d'un père sénégalais et d'une mère guadeloupéenne, elle connut très tôt la géographie intime de l'entre-deux. C'est par la recherche — sur les religions africaines en diaspora — qu'elle trouva le chemin vers Porto-Novo. Ce qui devait être un terrain de thèse devint un retour. Elle reçut son Fâ. Elle fut initiée au Vodoun. Elle déposa ses notes et apprit à écouter.

Mariétou s'adresse aux francophones de la diaspora africaine et caribéenne — à ceux qui portent la question de leurs origines sans savoir encore par où commencer à la dérouler.

How it works

Four steps.
No agenda.

01
You reach out

Use the contact form to request a conversation. Tell us your language and which Bridge you'd like to speak with. No need to explain yourself — the request itself is enough.

02
We connect you

The Bridge receives your request and confirms a time within a few days. The conversation happens by video or phone, wherever you are in the world.

03
Thirty minutes

The conversation is yours. There is no curriculum, no sales pitch, no assessment. The Bridge listens. They may share their own experience where it's relevant. You speak freely.

04
You decide what's next

Nothing is proposed, nothing is required. If the conversation opens a door you want to walk through, Ayo Traditions exists. But the conversation has no condition, no continuation, no obligation.

Our commitments

A sacred space,
not a sales channel.

  • Total confidentiality. What you share in a Living Bridge conversation stays there. It is not stored, not analysed, not used for marketing.
  • No recording without consent. If the conversation is recorded for training purposes, you will be asked — clearly, before it begins. You may refuse. The conversation will be exactly the same.
  • No referral pressure. The Bridge will not mention Ayo Traditions programmes unless you ask. Their role is not to convert — it is to listen.
  • Clear limits. If what you share requires professional support — psychological, medical, legal — the Bridge will say so, and will offer resources. They hold space, not expertise they do not have.
  • The Bridge is also in process. They are not finished. They are not perfect. They are on the path — which is exactly why they can accompany others on it.
For those who have crossed

Are you a Living Bridge?

The Living Bridge programme is not open to applications in the ordinary sense. It is a recognition — offered by Ayo Traditions to afro-descendants who have completed a significant portion of their personal return path, received initiation within a West African tradition, and demonstrate the character of those described in the Ayo Coach Charter: a witness, not a saviour. A gardener, not a healer.

If you believe you may carry this, and if you speak a language in which the diaspora has not yet been served — we are listening.
